Our story

A long walk
home, together.

We met as friends-of-friends in Bangalore, traded book recommendations for two years, and somewhere along the way stopped pretending it was only that. Here is the short version.

  1. 2022

    A rainy evening in Indiranagar

    A mutual friend's birthday dinner, a power cut, and a four-hour conversation by candlelight about libraries, mothers and the merits of Kerala parotta. Neither of us called the next day.

  2. 2024

    Munnar, in the off-season

    We took a long weekend up to the hills with friends. Prasanth forgot a jacket. Sharon shared hers. The trip is also where we first said the thing out loud.

  3. 2025

    The proposal, Fort Kochi

    At the seawall just after sunset, in front of the Chinese fishing nets. Prasanth had been carrying the ring for nine days. Sharon had been waiting for him to stop carrying it.

In between

Make a week
of it.

Kerala is best taken slowly. If you’ve come this far, stay a few extra days — these are the four places we’d send our closest friends.

Fort Kochi

01 — history

Colonial streets, the spice market, a Jewish quarter, and the Chinese fishing nets. Half a day, comfortably.

Alleppey

02 — water

Houseboats through the backwaters. Stay one night on the water — you’ll come back recalibrated.

Munnar

03 — hills

Tea estates that fold into clouds. Cool nights, cardamom in the air, and the best two-day reset.

Varkala

04 — coast

A clifftop beach town an hour from Kollam. Quiet cafés, decent surf and the kind of sunsets you write about.

Good to know

What should I wear?
For the engagement — festive Indian or smart-casual; sarees, kurtas, suits all welcome. For the wedding — formal or saree; the mass is in a church, so shoulders covered please. Light fabrics. Kerala in August is humid.
What’s the weather like?
Tail end of the monsoon. Expect 26–30 °C, high humidity, and afternoon showers most days. Pack a small umbrella and breathable clothes.
What language will be spoken?
Malayalam and English at the engagement; the wedding mass is part Malayalam, part Latin, part English. Programmes will be printed in English.
What kind of food will be served?
Delicious traditional Kerala cuisine, including vegetarian and non-vegetarian options.
