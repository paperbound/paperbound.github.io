Ideas on Learning and Memory

People perform this or that kind of learning throughout their lives for all sorts of reasons, to keep up with their interests, maintaining social relationships, navigating the daily routines of their lives, etc. Learning in this sense is as quotidian an activity as dining, however people engage in this activity in more intricate ways - either by the nature of their jobs (so called knowledge workers) or simply their own curiosity. There is also the matter of the different disciplines in learning - unstructured learning over considerable time intervals in varied domains, startegic skill making through apprenticeship, or self-learning over a smaller period of time and more. Regardless of the subject, there should be general principles, approaches, and strategy one hopes to use that can reduce the anxiety and risk involved in trying to learn.
